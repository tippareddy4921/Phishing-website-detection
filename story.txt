Into the Shoes of Abhimanyu: Navigating the Chakravyuha

It was the 13th day of the Kurukshetra War. My father, Arjun, was away fighting on the battlefield, and tension filled the air among the Pandavas. Dronacharya , the commander of the Kauravas, had created a tough battle formation called the Chakravyuha. No one in our army knew how to break through it.

But I was different.

From the time I was in my mother Subhadra’s womb, I learned how to dismantle this formation. My father had explained the strategy to her, and Krishna, who was like a guide and mentor to me , had also taught me how to break the Chakravyuha. However, what I didn’t know was how to escape once I got inside.
As I prepared to face this challenge, I urged my fellow Pandavas to follow me into the formation. I believed we could succeed together, but they hesitated, unsure about a young boy taking on such a daunting task. Despite their doubts, I felt a strong determination to lead them into battle.

I was on my horse, armed with a bow, arrows,bunch of spears and equipped with my knowledge of how to break into the Chakravyuha.

As I got near the Chakravyuha, I felt scared and excited. This wasn't just a group of soldiers standing still. It was like a living thing, made to trap anyone who came in. I knew I had to be brave and smart to make it through.
Going Through the Circle
When I entered, I saw so many warriors standing close together, their shields making a tight wall. The big circle they made looked amazing but scary. I held my horse's reins tight, ready to face this dangerous maze.

At first, I rode along the inside of the circle. The ground was bumpy with stuff from earlier fights, so I couldn’t just charge ahead. I looked for weak spots in the enemy’s defense. I knew the soldiers were ready to fight back at any time.
Finding Times to Break Through
As I went around, my heart beat fast. I held my spear ready, looking for any chance to attack. When a soldier stumbled or turned away, I took my chance. Every time I knocked down an enemy, it caused some confusion. But I knew it wouldn't last long. They would quickly get back in line.

Every time I broke through, new soldiers would take the place of the ones I beat. It was like the formation was alive, always changing to keep me in. I realized I needed to do more than just be fast. I had to hit hard and use the short moments of confusion I created.
Changing How I Fought
I started switching between going around and charging in suddenly. This kept the enemy guessing. Each time I broke through a layer, I could feel how hard they were all trying to stop me.

When I charged through with my spear, I quickly looked for the next gap. New warriors would move in right away to close the gap I'd made. Their shields locked together again, making a new wall to stop me.
I had to act fast. With every hit, I pushed forward, knowing the soldiers would quickly surround me again. I tried not just to break their lines but to make them unsure and hesitate.

Getting Deeper In
As I got further in, everything was moving. Soldiers were always shifting to keep their formation strong. I was in the middle of it all, fighting hard with my body and mind.

With each layer I broke through, the walls of soldiers closed in tighter. I could feel their shields pressing on me. My heart raced as they regrouped quickly. Each new wall was a challenge, but I couldn't let it scare me.
I swung my spear in big circles, aiming at the weak spots. The soldiers reacted fast, but I managed to make small openings. I was fighting not just the soldiers but the whole Chakravyuha itself.

My plan mixed smart thinking with strong determination. At first, I went in circles to check things out. But as I got deeper, I knew I had to act fast. The soldiers behind me would quickly close off my way out.
To get through, I mixed going in circles with sudden charges, always trying to stay ahead. My goal was clear: to reach Duryodhana and capture him.

Reaching the Center
When I finally got to the middle of the Chakravyuha, my heart was racing. I saw Duryodhana surrounded by his strongest warriors. I knew getting out would be even harder than getting in.

Realizing the Trap
As soon as I charged at Duryodhana, his guards made a tight wall around him. I couldn't get through with my spear or sword. I quickly realized I couldn't capture Duryodhana here. I had to turn back and find a way out.

But when I turned, I saw that the soldiers had closed ranks behind me. Every part of the Chakravyuha had closed in. The paths I'd taken to get in were now even tighter, trapping me.
Trying to Escape
I tried to go back the way I came, but the soldiers I'd beaten were replaced by new ones. They regrouped even faster now. I hit at the soldiers blocking my way out, but more kept coming. The Chakravyuha was made to keep me in, not let me out.

Every time I made a gap, more soldiers filled it. I started to see that the Chakravyuha wasn't just a wall of people, but a clever trap. It was almost impossible to leave once you were deep inside. My horse, which had helped me get in, now couldn't move well in the chaos.
Planning to Get Out
I knew I had to use my instincts and speed. My only hope was to break through one of the circles again, but this time, I had to be really fast before they could regroup. I charged at a weak spot, aiming for soldiers who seemed distracted. I swung my sword as hard as I could, breaking through a small part of the circle.

But as soon as I moved forward, the soldiers closed in behind me again. They kept regrouping, leaving me no clear way out. It was like being in a maze where the walls kept moving, blocking any path I tried to take.
The Endless Attack
More and more soldiers came at me, but it was the great warriors who were the real problem. Dronacharya's army was so strong, it felt impossible to beat. I knew I couldn't win against all of them, but I had to keep fighting. I was careful with every move, but I was being attacked from all sides.

The soldiers had regrouped, making the circle behind me tight. There was no way to escape. I was completely surrounded, trapped in this spinning, tight formation. The space around me got smaller, and the attacks came faster. It felt like the walls of soldiers were closing in, and all I could do was keep fighting.

With no other weapon strong enough, I used my chariot wheel chakra. I spun it fast, using it to block arrows, spears, and swords. But it wasn't enough. The strength of the army's attacks and Dronacharya's precise hits started to wear me down. My body hurt, but I didn't stop.

I felt like I was going to lose, but I kept fighting as hard as I could. My chakra was spinning so fast you could barely see it, but even that couldn't stop all the attacks. With each second, I was pushed deeper into the heart of the Chakravyuha, the deadly formation that had killed so many before me.
The Last Fight
As I stood in the middle of all this chaos, with my chakra broken and my body hurt badly, I knew I was near the end. I was so weak from all the hits, I could barely stand. I had fought with all my strength, but there were too many enemies, and they were too strong.


Karna came towards me, looking steady, his sword shining in the harsh light. I knew this was it - I couldn't escape or win. As I dropped my last weapon, too weak to hold it, I looked right at Karna. I could see he understood how much I was suffering.

Without waiting, Karna raised his sword. His strike was quick, but not cruel. At that moment, I realized he wasn't just ending my fight - he was ending my pain. The hurt that had been burning through me suddenly stopped.


With that last hit, Karna freed me from the pain of battle, from being trapped in the Chakravyuha with enemies all around. My vision got blurry, and I felt lighter as everything faded away.
