Once upon a time, deep inside a magical, emerald-green forest, lived a little bear named Barnaby. Barnaby was a very happy cub, and he had a giant toy chest filled with the shiniest spinning tops, the softest bouncing balls, and the most beautifully painted wooden blocks.Barnaby loved his toys, but he didn't like to share them. Whenever the other forest animals—like Mia the rabbit or Pip the squirrel—came by to play, Barnaby would hug his toy chest tightly and say, "Mine, all mine!"One sunny afternoon, a new little bear moved to the forest. His name was Toby. Toby was very friendly and had no toys of his own yet. He walked up to Barnaby and asked politely, "Can we play together?"Barnaby frowned and held onto his blocks. "No," he said.Later that afternoon, Barnaby was trying to build a super tall tower out of his wooden blocks. But every time he stacked three blocks high, the tower would wobble and fall down with a loud crash! Barnaby felt frustrated and sad. He sat on the grass and let out a big sigh.From behind a tree, Toby watched Barnaby struggle. Instead of being upset about earlier, Toby walked over with a big smile. He held out a shiny red bouncing ball and said, "Bouncing a ball is lots of fun. Would you like to try?"Barnaby’s eyes widened. He took the ball, threw it to Toby, and laughed as it bounced high into the air! They played and chased the ball until their tummies rumbled.Barnaby realized that having toys was great, but playing with a friend was even better. He ran over to his big toy chest, opened the lid, and pulled out his favorite spinning top. He handed it to Toby and said, "Here, you can play with this!"
